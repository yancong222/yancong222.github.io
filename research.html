Conferences / Workshops

    • Cong, Y.. (2021). Informal talk at ''Formal Linguistics and Philosophy (FLaP)'', The Hong Kong Baptist University.

    Poster-handout can be found here [URL]

    • Cong, Y. and Buccola, Brian. (2021). Poster Presentation of The 45th Annual Penn Linguistics Conference (PLC-45) on ''Alternatives in non-scalar implicature: The case of Mandarin adjective constructions'', University of Pennsylvania.

    This study investigates ambiguity from the perspective of Gricean pragmatics. Poster is available here [URL]

    • Cong, Y. (2019). Poster Presentation of Linguistic Society of America (LSA) Linguistics Institute on "Information structure and the depth of language processing — a pilot study", University of California at Davis.

    I extend my analysis of predicates of personal taste (words like tasty and beautiful) to epistemic modals (words like must). I showed that there is difference in processing times in different kinds of subjective predicates.

    • Smith, K., Ma, Y., Cong, Y., & Beretta, A. (2019). Presentation of the Psycholinguistics in Iceland—Parsing and Prediction (PiPP-2019) on "Interactions between syntax and semantics: The semantic P600", University of Iceland.

    We looked at sentences such as The cheese ate the mouse, The hearty meal was devouring the kids, and The dusty tabletops were devouring the kids. We find that evidence for an independent semantic analyzer comes from semantic role reversals.

    • Smith, K., Ma, Y., Cong, Y., & Beretta, A. (2019). Presentation of the 32nd CUNY Conference on Human Sentence Processing (CUNY-2019) on "Semantic attraction in sentence processing", University of Colorado Boulder. abstract

    Experiment 1 is a direct replication of Kim and Osterhout (2005). Experiment 2 is a conceptual replication of K&O (removing auxiliaries), in which we find when the preceding auxiliary was removed, the semantic P600 was eliminated.

    • Cong, Y. (2019). Presentation of the International Workshop on Degrees and Grammar: An East Asian Perspective (DeG-2019 ) on "A subjective analysis of the Mandarin ‘you’ structure", Nanjing University, China.

    I studied Chinese you constructions (e.g., Kai you zhihui meaning Kai has wisdom). I find diagnostics for predicates of personal taste in Mandarin Chinese.

    • Cong, Y. (2018). Presentation of the 12th Arizona Linguistics Circle (ALC-12 ) on "Rethinking the implicit argument in Kiswahili", University of Arizona.

    I looked at the stative morpheme -ik- in Kiswahili. I characterized and analyzed its feature syntactically.

    • Cong, Y. (2018). Poster Presentation of the 2nd Crete Linguistics Summer School (CreteLing-2) on "A preliminary semantic analysis of Predicates of Taste in Chinese", University of Crete.

    I extended anlysis of English find to Mandarin Chinese. Diagnostics are applied and subjectivity morphemes are identified in Chinese.

    • Cong, Y. (2017). Poster Presentation of Linguistic Society of America (LSA) Linguistics Institute on "T1 and T0 Sandhi in Tongliao Mandarin: an OT approach", University of Kentucky.

    I studied tonal features of my mother tongue Tongliao Mandarin using Optimality Theory.

    • Parrish, A., Kelley, P., Smith, K., Cong, Y., & Beretta, A. (2017). Presentation of the Society for the Neurobiology of Language (SNL) Annual Meeting on "Prosodic lengthening and boundary prediction in nominal compounds: An ERP study", at Maryland. abstract

    We studied perceptual and processing asymmetry in phonemic lengthening. We find that people are able to perceive lengthening far easier than they are able to perceive shortening.

    • Cong, Y. (2016). Presentation and Proceedings of the 28th North American Conference on Chinese Linguistics (NACCL-28) on "Markedness theory and the acquisition of the Mandarin potential complement construction", Brigham Young University.

    I studied L2 acquisition of a Chinese construction. I find that the ease of L2 acquisition is associated with target constructions' markedness feature.
