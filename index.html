Hello!

I am a postdoctoral trainee at the Feinstein Institute for Medical Research. I obtained my PhD in Linguistics from Michigan State University in August 2021, with an additional specialization in Cognitive Science. I'm interested in language meaning, specifically, the enriched meaning of degree constructions in context (e.g., adjectives such as tall).

Interests

My main interests are semantics and pragmatics. This means I study natural language meanings and how language users reason about meanings. Computational models, neurophysiology equipment, and behavioral experiments are used in my investigation. Projects details can be found in GitHub scripts and in my Resume.

Currently, I am using language models and natural language processing (NLP) to better understand psychiatric disorders. In general, we seek to understand the brain changes through the lens of language, so that preventions can be developed. In particular, our projects explore NLP methods for digitalizing clinically meaningful speech and language biomarkers for schizophrenia spectrum disorders. Projects details can be found in GitHub scriptscz.
