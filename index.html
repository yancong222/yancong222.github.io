Hello!

My work focuses on language in humans and machines. I address research questions on linguistics, natural language processing (NLP), and cognitive science. I am committed to developing text and speech analysis tools to quantify and understand language learning. Apart from linguistics, I enjoy engaging in outdoor activities.

Previous training and current research

I completed my PhD in Linguistics from Michigan State University in August 2021, with an additional specialization in Cognitive Science. Since then, I have been a postdoctoral researcher at the Feinstein Institutes. I also hold an MA in Second Language Acquisition. A quick summary can be found in my Resume.

Currently, I'm using (Large) Language Models and NLP to quantify neuropsychiatric disorders. In general, we seek to understand mental illness through the lens of speech and language. In particular, we focus on computational approaches to speech and language disorders. Projects details can be found in GitHub scriptscz.
