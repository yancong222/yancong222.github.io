Yan Cong

Hello!

I am a PhD candidate in the Linguistics program at Michigan State University, with an additional specialization in Cognitive Science. I am interested in language meaning and language in use. Currently I am investigating the (enriched) meaning of degree constructions (adjectives such as "expensive") in context.

Interests

My main interests are semantics and pragmatics, among other things. My dissertation aims are characterizing and explaining ambiguity/underspecification pragmatically. I study how speakers reason about meanings and how listeners reason about utterances. For example, by using "I will invite Anna or Kai", the speaker typically implies they won't invite both, even though strictly speaking, the sentence is compatible with them inviting both. This inference (implicature) arises because "or" competes with "and": the listener reasons that if the speaker were going to invite both, they would have said "I will invite Anna and Kai", which is more informative/specific. Thus, "or" is routinely enriched to mean "not both".

With regards to cognitive science, I am looking at how sound features such as rhythm and sonority influence speech perception in noise environment.
