Hello!

I'm a postdoctoral researcher at the Northwell Health Feinstein Institutes. I obtained my PhD in Linguistics from Michigan State University in August 2021, with an additional specialization in Cognitive Science. I'm interested in language meaning, specifically, the enriched meaning of degree constructions in context (e.g., adjectives such as tall).

Interests

My main interests are semantics and pragmatics. This means I study natural language meaning, meaning in context, and how language users reason about meaning. Computational models, neurophysiology equipment, and behavioral experiments are used in my investigation. A quick summary can be found in GitHub scripts and in my Resume.

I'm a linguist by training, a technologist by profession. I used to be a language teacher. I did my MA in language studies (emphasis in L2 acquisition), and BA in Teaching Chinese as a Foreign Language. My linguistics/language teaching experience has come from Mainland China, Hong Kong, and the United States.

Currently, I'm using (Large) Language Models and Natural Language Processing (NLP) to better understand neuropsychiatric disorders. In general, we seek to understand mental illness through the lens of speech and language. In particular, our projects focus on speech and language measures. Projects details can be found in GitHub scriptscz.
